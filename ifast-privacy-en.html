iFast Privacy Policy

Updated: July 2, 2026

We respect your privacy. This policy explains how iFast handles your information and how you can manage it.

1. Information We Handle

Information you enter or generate in the app may include your name or nickname, gender, birth date or age, height, current weight, starting weight, target weight, fasting plan, fasting start and end times, fasting notes, weight records, step display data, appearance settings, and notification settings.

If you send a bug report email, it may include the issue description you wrote and diagnostic information such as device model, system version, and app version.

2. Apple Health Data

Only after you explicitly authorize it will iFast read weight and step data from Apple Health to show weight trends and daily steps. It may also write weight data when you allow it.

Apple Health authorization is managed by the system. You can revoke permission at any time in system settings or the Apple Health app. iFast does not sell Apple Health data to third parties and does not use it for advertising tracking.

3. Local Storage and Sync

iFast currently stores most data locally on your device. For widgets and Apple Watch sync, necessary fasting state may be written to an App Group or transferred between your devices through WatchConnectivity.

iCloud Sync is currently shown as not available. While it is unavailable, iFast does not actively upload your fasting records, weight records, or profile data to iCloud.

4. Purchase Information

Tip purchases are processed by the Apple App Store. iFast obtains product information, purchase results, and transaction verification status from StoreKit to show prices, complete purchases, and restore purchases.

We do not directly collect or store your card number, Apple ID password, or full payment credentials in the app. Refunds, billing, and payment security are handled by Apple.

5. Third-Party Sharing

iFast does not include third-party advertising SDKs and does not sell your health data, fasting records, or weight records to third parties.

When you use Apple Health, App Store purchases, email feedback, widgets, or Apple Watch, related data is handled according to Apple system-service rules on your device or between your Apple devices.

6. Retention and Deletion

Local data remains on your device until you delete it in the app, uninstall the app, or clear related data through the system.

You can use Delete Current Data on the Me page to clear fasting records, weight records, profile data, and current settings. You can also revoke Apple Health permission in system settings.

7. Children

iFast is not intended for children under 13 and does not encourage minors to fast without guidance from guardians and professionals.

8. Contact
